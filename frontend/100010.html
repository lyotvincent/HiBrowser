Step One
example figure 1



  • Load MCF-6 on the first viewer
  • Load MCF-10A on the second viewer
  • Load MCF-6(Contact Map) and MCF-10A(Control Map) on the third viewer

(1) Hi-C analyses identify that small chromosomes (chr16–22) in the MCF10A genome show preferential associations with each other.

(2) Significant interactions within and between chr16–22 are shown by using the Control and Contact Map Function.

(3) The 4th Viewer show the first principal component of MCF-6 and MCF-10A separately.

Step Two



(1) On the 1st Viewer, load A/B compartment files(.bigwig) of MCF-6 and MCF-10A.

(2) Open the analyses page by clicking widget icon

(3) Click the buton Display.

(4) The differential compartments are defined as genomic regions in which one type of compartmentalization is observed in one cell line and the other compartment type in the second cell line.

(5) The red area indicates the unchanged area, while the gray area indicates the difference area.

Step Three
example figure 3





(1) On the 1st Viewer, load TAD files(.txt) of MCF-6 and MCF-10A.

(2) On the pannel, Click the and modify the two tracks to different colors

(3) TADs are similar between MCF-10A and MCF-7 genomes.

Step Four

(1) Open the analyses page by clicking widget icon

(2) On this page We can see:

  • The violin diagram showing the distribution of TAD on each chromosome
  • Two column bar chart showing the difference of TAD between MCF-6 and MCF-10A.
  • Pie chart showing the genomic compartment changes between MCF-10A and MCF-7 genomes. "A" and "B" denote the open and closed compartments, respectively.
    • "A→A" represents compartments that are open in both cell lines
    • "B→B" represents compartments that are closed in both cell lines
    • "A→B" denotes compartments that are open in MCF-10A but closed in MCF-7
    • "B→A" denotes compartments that are closed in MCF-10A and open in MCF-7

Click the chart to view the details on the right.
